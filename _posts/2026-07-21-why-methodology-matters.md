title: "Why Methodology Matters in Cybersecurity"
date: 2026-07-21
---

When I started learning penetration testing, I thought that it was mostly about tools, knowing Metasploit, memorizing Nmap flags, running the right exploit at the right time. It took me a while to realize that tools are the easy part. Methodology is what actually makes you effective.

I first felt this gap during a CTF and lab work. I had all the tools I needed, but I was jumping between challenges with no real process, scanning one target, then getting distracted by another, then circling back and forgetting what I'd already tried. I wasted time repeating steps and missed things that were sitting in plain sight.

Now, whether I'm working through a PJPT lab or testing in a live environment, I treat methodology as the foundation everything else sits on. The tools change constantly. The process is what stays consistent, and it's the difference between finding a vulnerability by luck and finding it because you knew where to look.

If you're starting out in this field, my advice: learn the process before you fall in love with the tools. The tools will make more sense once you do.
